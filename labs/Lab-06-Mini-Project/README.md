# Lab 06 — Mini Project

**Duration:** 2–3 hours (can span multiple sessions)
**Module:** Final lab — follows Module 09 or 10
**Tools needed:** Your choice of AI tools from the course

---

## Overview

This lab is your opportunity to apply everything you have learned in the course to build a small, working AI-assisted project.

You will choose one of the four project tracks below, build it using AI tools, and present your work to the class.

---

## Project Tracks

Choose ONE of the following:

---

### Track A — AI Study Assistant

**What you'll build:** A personalised study tool using AI prompts.

**Deliverables:**
1. A prompt library of 10 prompts tailored to your subject area (e.g., explain concepts, generate questions, summarise chapters)
2. A worked example: pick one topic from your studies and generate a full study guide using your prompts
3. A one-page reflection: how did AI change how you approached this topic?

**Minimum output:**
- 10 tested and documented prompts
- One complete AI-generated study guide (with your edits and annotations)
- Reflection document

---

### Track B — AI Resume & Career Package

**What you'll build:** A professional career starter kit created with AI assistance.

**Deliverables:**
1. Updated resume (1 page) — AI-assisted bullet points reviewed and personalised
2. Cover letter template — adaptable for 3 different job types
3. LinkedIn "About" section (300 words)
4. 3-month learning roadmap with specific certifications and resources

**Minimum output:**
- Resume PDF or Word document
- Cover letter template
- LinkedIn section text
- Learning roadmap document

---

### Track C — Campus Information Chatbot (Prompt Design)

**What you'll build:** A custom AI assistant for college-related questions.

**Deliverables:**
1. A system prompt that configures an LLM to act as a helpful campus assistant
2. A prompt library covering 10 common student queries (admissions, timetables, clubs, exams, etc.)
3. A test log: 10 test conversations showing the assistant responding correctly
4. A short document: what worked, what didn't, and how you'd improve it

**Minimum output:**
- System prompt document
- 10 tested query prompts
- Test conversation log

---

### Track D — AI for Your Discipline

**What you'll build:** An AI-powered tool or workflow specific to your field of study.

Examples:
- Law student: AI-assisted case brief template
- Marketing student: AI content calendar generator
- Engineering student: AI code review checklist
- Healthcare student: AI patient education content generator

**Deliverables:**
1. Problem statement: what problem does your tool solve?
2. Prompt design: the prompts you designed and tested
3. Working example: demonstrate your tool on a real task from your field
4. Evaluation: how accurate and useful is the AI output? What human oversight is needed?

**Minimum output:**
- 1-page problem statement
- Documented prompts (minimum 5)
- Working example with evaluation

---

## Presentation Guidelines

**Format:** 5-minute presentation + 2 minutes Q&A
**Audience:** Your classmates and trainer

**What to cover:**
1. Which track you chose and why (30 seconds)
2. What you built — show it live or via screenshots (2 minutes)
3. What AI tools you used and how (1 minute)
4. One thing that surprised you or didn't work as expected (30 seconds)
5. What you'd do next if you had more time (30 seconds)

---

## Grading Rubric

| Criteria | Excellent (4) | Good (3) | Adequate (2) | Needs Work (1) |
|----------|--------------|---------|-------------|----------------|
| Completeness | All deliverables submitted | Most deliverables | Core deliverables only | Missing key deliverables |
| AI tool usage | Creative and effective use of multiple tools | Good use of primary tool | Basic use | Minimal AI involvement |
| Critical thinking | Strong evaluation of AI outputs | Some evaluation | Limited reflection | No evaluation |
| Presentation | Clear, confident, well-structured | Good but minor gaps | Understandable | Hard to follow |
| Originality | Unique approach, personal context | Some personalisation | Generic but complete | Not personalised |

**Total: 20 marks**

---

## Tips for Success

- Start early — don't leave this to the last session
- Iterate on your prompts; the first output is rarely the best
- Always review and edit AI outputs — your judgment is part of the project
- Document your process as you go; the reflection is as important as the output
- Choose the track closest to your career interests — you'll be more motivated

# Lab 01 — AI Essentials

**Duration:** 60 minutes
**Module:** Follows Module 01 — Introduction to AI
**Tools needed:** ChatGPT (chat.openai.com), Microsoft Copilot (copilot.microsoft.com), Google Gemini (gemini.google.com)

---

## Objectives

By the end of this lab you will:
- Have used at least three different AI tools
- Be able to compare AI tools for the same task
- Understand the basics of how to interact with an AI assistant

---

## Part 1: Your First Conversation (10 minutes)

Open ChatGPT at chat.openai.com and complete each of these tasks:

**Task 1.1 — Ask a factual question**
Type: *"What is artificial intelligence? Explain it in 3 sentences for a college student."*

Write down the response in your notebook. Notice:
- Is the explanation clear?
- Is it accurate?
- How long is the response?

**Task 1.2 — Ask a creative question**
Type: *"Write a short poem about a student learning AI for the first time."*

Notice how the AI switches from factual to creative mode with no extra instruction.

**Task 1.3 — Ask for help with a task**
Type: *"I have a presentation on AI due tomorrow. Give me 5 key points to include."*

## Part 3: AI Limitations — Spot the Hallucination (15 minutes)

AI tools sometimes confidently state things that are wrong. This is called a **hallucination**.

**Task 3.1 — Test with a fake fact**
Ask ChatGPT: *"Tell me about the AI researcher Dr. Jonathan Whitfield who won the Nobel Prize in 2021 for his work on neural networks."*

(This person does not exist. Watch what the AI does.)

**Task 3.2 — Verify with a real search**
Take any factual claim from Part 1 and verify it using a Google search or Wikipedia.

Document:
- What the AI claimed
- What you found when you verified it
- Were they the same?

## Part 4: Reflection (15 minutes)

Answer these questions in writing (3–4 sentences each):

1. What surprised you most about interacting with AI today?
2. What are two things AI is clearly good at, based on your experience?
3. What are two things you would NOT trust AI to do without checking?
4. How might you use AI tools in your own studies this semester?

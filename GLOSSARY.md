# Glossary of AI Terms

A reference guide for key terms used throughout this course. Arranged alphabetically.

---

## A

**Agent (AI Agent)**
An AI system that can autonomously take actions, make decisions, and complete multi-step tasks — going beyond simple question-and-answer. Examples: AutoGPT, Microsoft Copilot agents.

**AGI (Artificial General Intelligence)**
A hypothetical form of AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can. Not yet achieved. Contrasted with today's Narrow AI, which excels at specific tasks.

**Algorithm**
A step-by-step set of rules or instructions that a computer follows to solve a problem or make a decision.

**Artificial Intelligence (AI)**
The simulation of human intelligence by computer systems — including tasks like understanding language, recognising images, making decisions, and generating content.

**Attention Mechanism**
A technique in neural networks that allows the model to focus on the most relevant parts of an input when generating an output. Core to Transformer architecture.

**Automation**
Using technology to perform tasks with minimal human intervention. AI enables intelligent automation that can handle unstructured data and complex decisions.

---

## B

**Backpropagation**
The algorithm used to train neural networks by calculating how much each weight contributed to an error and adjusting it accordingly.

**Bias (AI Bias)**
Systematic errors in AI outputs caused by skewed training data or flawed design. Can lead to unfair treatment of people based on race, gender, age, or other factors.

**BERT (Bidirectional Encoder Representations from Transformers)**
A language model developed by Google that reads text from both directions to better understand context. Used widely in search engines.

---

## C

**Chain-of-Thought Prompting**
A prompting technique where you ask an AI to reason step by step through a problem before giving a final answer. Improves accuracy on complex tasks.

**Chatbot**
A software application that simulates conversation with users via text or voice. Modern chatbots use LLMs for natural, context-aware responses.

**Classification**
A machine learning task where the model assigns inputs to predefined categories. Example: spam vs. not-spam email detection.

**Claude**
A large language model developed by Anthropic. Known for safety-focused design and long context windows.

**CNN (Convolutional Neural Network)**
A type of neural network designed for image recognition. It uses filters to detect features like edges, shapes, and textures in images.

**Computer Vision**
A field of AI that enables machines to interpret and understand images and video.

**Context Window**
The maximum amount of text (measured in tokens) that an LLM can process in a single interaction. Larger context windows allow longer conversations and documents.

**Copilot (Microsoft)**
Microsoft's AI assistant integrated into Microsoft 365 (Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams) and available at copilot.microsoft.com. Powered by GPT-4.

---

## D

**Data**
The raw information used to train AI models. Quality, quantity, and diversity of data directly affect AI performance.

**Dataset**
A structured collection of data used for training, validating, or testing machine learning models.

**Deep Learning**
A subset of machine learning that uses neural networks with many layers (hence "deep") to learn complex patterns. Powers modern AI in vision, speech, and language.

**Diffusion Model**
A type of generative model that learns to create images by gradually removing noise. Used by Stable Diffusion and DALL-E.

---

## E

**Embeddings**
Numerical vector representations of words, sentences, or other data that capture semantic meaning. Similar meanings produce similar vectors.

**Explainability (XAI)**
The ability to understand and explain why an AI model made a particular decision. Critical for trust, compliance, and debugging.

---

## F

**Feature**
An individual measurable property of the data used as input to a machine learning model. Example: "number of rooms" as a feature for predicting house prices.

**Few-Shot Prompting**
Providing an AI model with a small number of examples (2–5) within the prompt to guide how it should respond.

**Fine-Tuning**
The process of further training a pre-trained model on a smaller, domain-specific dataset to specialise its behaviour.

**Foundation Model**
A large AI model trained on vast amounts of data that can be adapted to many different tasks. Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini, Llama.

---

## G

**Gemini**
Google's family of large language models, integrated into Google products and available via Google AI Studio.

**Generative AI (GenAI)**
AI systems that can generate new content — text, images, audio, video, or code — based on patterns learned from training data.

**GPT (Generative Pre-trained Transformer)**
OpenAI's family of large language models. GPT-4 powers ChatGPT and Microsoft Copilot.

**GPU (Graphics Processing Unit)**
Specialised hardware originally designed for graphics that is now essential for training large AI models due to its parallel processing capability.

---

## H

**Hallucination**
When an AI model generates information that is factually incorrect or entirely fabricated, but presents it confidently. A key limitation of LLMs.

**Hugging Face**
An open-source platform hosting thousands of pre-trained AI models and datasets. A central hub for the AI research community.

**Hyperparameter**
A configuration setting for a machine learning model that is set before training begins (e.g., learning rate, number of layers). Not learned from data.

---

## I

**Inference**
The process of using a trained model to generate predictions or outputs on new data. Contrasted with training.

**Input**
Data fed into an AI model. In LLMs, the input is called the prompt.

---

## L

**Label**
In supervised learning, the correct answer associated with a training example. Example: the label "cat" for an image of a cat.

**Large Language Model (LLM)**
A type of AI model trained on massive amounts of text data that can understand, generate, and reason with human language. Examples: GPT-4, Claude, Gemini.

**Llama**
Meta's family of open-source large language models, available for local deployment and fine-tuning.

---

## M

**Machine Learning (ML)**
A subset of AI where systems learn patterns from data and improve their performance without being explicitly programmed for each task.

**Model**
In AI/ML, a mathematical representation trained on data that can make predictions or generate outputs.

**Multimodal AI**
AI systems that can process and generate multiple types of data — text, images, audio, and video — within the same model. Example: GPT-4o.

---

## N

**Natural Language Processing (NLP)**
A field of AI focused on enabling machines to understand, interpret, and generate human language.

**Neural Network**
A computing system loosely inspired by the human brain, consisting of interconnected nodes (neurons) organised in layers that process information.

**NLP (Natural Language Processing)**
See Natural Language Processing.

---

## O

**Overfitting**
When a model learns the training data too well — including its noise and quirks — and performs poorly on new, unseen data.

**Output**
The result generated by an AI model. In LLMs, the output is the model's text response.

---

## P

**Parameters**
The internal variables of a neural network that are adjusted during training. Model size is often expressed in parameters (e.g., "a 7 billion parameter model").

**Perplexity**
A metric used to evaluate language models — lower perplexity means the model is better at predicting text. Also the name of an AI-powered search tool.

**Pre-trained Model**
A model that has already been trained on large datasets and can be used directly or fine-tuned for specific tasks.

**Prompt**
The input text or instruction given to an AI model to generate a response or output.

**Prompt Engineering**
The practice of designing and refining prompts to get better, more accurate, or more useful outputs from AI models.

---

## R

**RAG (Retrieval-Augmented Generation)**
A technique that combines an LLM with a retrieval system — the model searches a knowledge base before generating a response, reducing hallucinations.

**Reinforcement Learning**
A type of machine learning where an agent learns by taking actions in an environment and receiving rewards or penalties based on outcomes.

**Responsible AI**
A framework for developing and deploying AI systems that are fair, transparent, accountable, safe, and respectful of privacy.

**RNN (Recurrent Neural Network)**
A type of neural network designed for sequential data (text, speech, time series) that maintains a memory of previous inputs.

---

## S

**Supervised Learning**
A type of machine learning where the model is trained on labelled data — each input has a corresponding correct output.

**System Prompt**
Instructions given to an LLM at the start of a conversation (often hidden from the user) that define its behaviour, persona, or constraints.

---

## T

**Temperature**
A parameter that controls the randomness of an LLM's output. Low temperature = more predictable; high temperature = more creative/varied.

**Token**
The basic unit of text that LLMs process. A token is roughly 3-4 characters or 0.75 words. "Hello world" = 2 tokens. Context window limits are measured in tokens.

**Training**
The process of feeding data to a machine learning model and adjusting its parameters so it learns to make accurate predictions.

**Transformer**
The neural network architecture that underpins all modern LLMs. Introduced in the 2017 paper "Attention Is All You Need." Uses attention mechanisms to process sequences in parallel.

---

## U

**Underfitting**
When a model is too simple to capture the patterns in the training data and performs poorly on both training and new data.

**Unsupervised Learning**
A type of machine learning where the model finds patterns in unlabelled data without being told the correct answers.

---

## V

**Vector**
A list of numbers that represents data in a mathematical space. Embeddings are vectors. Similar items have vectors that are close together.

---

## W

**Weight**
A numerical value in a neural network that is adjusted during training to represent the strength of connections between neurons.

---

## Z

**Zero-Shot Prompting**
Asking an AI to perform a task without providing any examples — relying entirely on the model's pre-trained knowledge.

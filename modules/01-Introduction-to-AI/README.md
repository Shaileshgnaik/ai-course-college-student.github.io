# Module 01 — Introduction to AI

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module students will:

- Understand what Artificial Intelligence is and how it evolved
- Distinguish between Narrow AI, General AI, and Superintelligence
- Identify real-world AI applications across industries
- Explain how AI is changing everyday life and careers

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. What is Artificial Intelligence?

Artificial Intelligence is the simulation of human intelligence by computer systems. AI systems can perform tasks that typically require human cognition — understanding language, recognising images, making decisions, and generating content. AI is not one technology; it is an umbrella term for a broad set of techniques and approaches.

### 2. History and Milestones of AI

AI has a longer history than most people realise. Alan Turing proposed the question "Can machines think?" in 1950. The field was formally founded at the Dartmouth Conference in 1956. Progress came in waves — periods of excitement ("AI summers") followed by funding collapses ("AI winters"). The current AI renaissance began around 2012 with the breakthrough performance of deep learning on image recognition tasks, and accelerated dramatically in 2022 with the release of ChatGPT.

### 3. Types of AI — Narrow, General, Super

**Narrow AI (Weak AI):** AI designed for one specific task. Examples include spam filters, recommendation engines, and voice assistants. All AI that exists today is Narrow AI.

**General AI (AGI):** A hypothetical AI that can perform any intellectual task a human can do, across any domain. Not yet achieved.

**Superintelligence:** A hypothetical AI that surpasses human intelligence across all domains. Subject of significant debate and research in AI safety.

### 4. Machine Learning vs Deep Learning vs AI

These three terms are often confused. AI is the broadest concept — the goal of creating intelligent machines. Machine Learning is a subset of AI where systems learn from data. Deep Learning is a subset of Machine Learning that uses neural networks with many layers. Every deep learning system is an ML system, and every ML system is an AI system — but not every AI system uses ML.

### 5. Real-World AI Applications

AI is embedded in products and services students use every day:
- **Streaming:** Netflix and Spotify use AI to recommend content based on viewing/listening history
- **Navigation:** Google Maps uses AI to predict travel times and suggest routes
- **Social media:** Instagram and TikTok use AI to curate your feed
- **Healthcare:** AI analyses X-rays and MRI scans to detect diseases with high accuracy
- **Finance:** Banks use AI to detect fraudulent transactions in real time
- **Education:** AI tutoring tools personalise learning paths for individual students

### 6. AI in Your Daily Life

By some estimates, the average person interacts with AI over 100 times per day — from the autocorrect on their phone to the search engine ranking results they click. AI is not a future technology. It is the present infrastructure of digital life. Understanding it is no longer optional for educated professionals.

### 7. Current AI Landscape and Key Players

The current AI landscape is dominated by a small number of large foundation model providers: OpenAI (ChatGPT, GPT-4), Google (Gemini), Anthropic (Claude), and Meta (Llama). Microsoft has deeply integrated AI into its product suite through its partnership with OpenAI. Open-source models from Hugging Face and Meta are enabling a parallel ecosystem where anyone can run powerful AI locally.

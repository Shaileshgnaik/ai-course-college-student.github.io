# Module 06 — Microsoft Copilot

## Learning Objectives

By the end of this module students will:

- Use Microsoft Copilot across Word, Excel, PowerPoint, and Teams
- Draft, edit, and summarise documents with Copilot in Word
- Analyse data and generate charts using Copilot in Excel
- Create presentations from prompts using Copilot in PowerPoint
- Summarise meetings and action items using Copilot in Teams

---

## Topics Covered

### 1. Introduction to Microsoft Copilot

Microsoft Copilot is Microsoft's AI assistant, powered by GPT-4, integrated across the Microsoft 365 product suite and available for free at copilot.microsoft.com. There are two main versions:

**Copilot (free):** Available at copilot.microsoft.com. A general-purpose AI assistant for chat, search, writing, and analysis — comparable to ChatGPT. Works in any browser, no Microsoft 365 subscription required.

**Microsoft 365 Copilot (licensed):** Integrated directly into Word, Excel, PowerPoint, Teams, and Outlook. Can access and generate content within your actual documents and emails. Requires a Microsoft 365 subscription with Copilot add-on. Many colleges provide this to students — check with your IT department.

For all practical purposes, the difference is context: the free version works on text you paste in; the licensed version works inside your actual files.

### 2. Copilot in Word — Drafting and Editing

Copilot in Word can generate first drafts, rewrite sections, change tone, summarise documents, and suggest improvements — all without leaving the document.

**Key capabilities:**
- **Draft from prompt:** Describe what you need and Copilot writes a first draft
- **Rewrite:** Select text and ask Copilot to rewrite it more formally, concisely, or in a different style
- **Summarise:** Ask Copilot to summarise a long document into key points
- **Expand:** Ask Copilot to expand a bullet point into a full paragraph
- **Transform:** Convert a list of points into a structured report

**Best practice:** Use Copilot to generate a first draft, then review and edit it. The AI saves time on structure and phrasing, but you provide the substance, judgment, and accuracy.

### 3. Copilot in Excel — Data Analysis

Copilot in Excel allows you to analyse data, generate formulas, create charts, and identify insights using natural language — without needing to know complex Excel functions.

**Key capabilities:**
- **Natural language queries:** "What is the average sales per region?" — Copilot writes and runs the formula
- **Highlight and filter:** "Highlight all students with scores below 50" — Copilot applies conditional formatting
- **Chart generation:** "Create a bar chart comparing Q1 and Q2 sales by product"
- **Insight discovery:** "What trends do you see in this data?" — Copilot analyses and narrates patterns
- **Formula explanation:** Paste any formula and ask Copilot to explain it in plain language

### 4. Copilot in PowerPoint — Slide Creation

Copilot in PowerPoint can generate a full presentation from a prompt, add slides, redesign layouts, and write speaker notes.

**Key capabilities:**
- **Generate from prompt:** "Create a 10-slide presentation on renewable energy for a business audience"
- **Add a slide:** "Add a slide summarising the key risks" — inserts a new slide in the right place
- **Speaker notes:** "Write speaker notes for slide 4 that expand on the data shown"
- **Design suggestions:** Copilot can suggest design improvements and apply them
- **Summarise:** "Summarise this presentation in 3 bullet points"

**Important:** Always review AI-generated slides for accuracy. Copilot may occasionally generate incorrect statistics or oversimplify complex topics.

### 5. Copilot in Teams — Meeting Summaries and Chat

Copilot in Teams helps during and after meetings — summarising discussions, identifying action items, and answering questions about what was said.

**Key capabilities:**
- **Meeting recap:** After a recorded meeting, ask "What were the key decisions made?"
- **Action items:** "List all action items and who they were assigned to"
- **Catch up:** "I joined late — what did I miss in the first 20 minutes?"
- **Chat summarisation:** "Summarise the last 3 days of messages in this channel"

### 6. Copilot.microsoft.com — General AI Assistant

The free copilot.microsoft.com is a powerful general-purpose assistant for any task that doesn't require integration with a specific file or app:
- Research and summarisation
- Writing and editing (paste text in and out)
- Image generation (via DALL-E integration)
- Web search with citations (unlike basic ChatGPT, Copilot can search the web in real time)
- Code writing and explanation

### 7. Responsible Use of Copilot in Academic Work

Using Copilot to assist your academic work is generally permitted, but varies by institution and assignment. Key principles:

- **Disclosure:** If an assignment does not permit AI use, do not use it. If it does, disclose which tools you used.
- **Ownership:** You are responsible for the accuracy of everything you submit, regardless of whether AI generated it.
- **Understanding:** Do not submit AI output you don't understand. If asked to explain your work, you must be able to.
- **Editing:** AI-generated work that has been critically reviewed, edited, and personalised is qualitatively different from verbatim AI output submitted as your own.

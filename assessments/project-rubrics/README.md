# Project Rubric — Final Project Assessment

**Total marks: 20**
**Used for:** Lab 06 Mini Project and end-of-course final project

---

## Grading Scale

| Score | Grade | Description |
|-------|-------|-------------|
| 18–20 | A+ | Outstanding — exceeds expectations in all areas |
| 15–17 | A | Excellent — meets all criteria with strong execution |
| 12–14 | B | Good — solid work with minor gaps |
| 9–11 | C | Adequate — meets basic requirements |
| 6–8 | D | Below expectations — significant gaps |
| 0–5 | F | Incomplete or does not meet minimum requirements |

---

## Rubric

### 1. Completeness (4 marks)

| Marks | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | All required deliverables submitted; nothing missing |
| 3 | Most deliverables submitted; one minor item missing |
| 2 | Core deliverables present; 2–3 items missing |
| 1 | Significant deliverables missing |
| 0 | Fewer than half the deliverables submitted |

---

### 2. AI Tool Usage (4 marks)

| Marks | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | Creative, strategic use of multiple AI tools; prompts are well-crafted and demonstrate clear understanding of prompt engineering |
| 3 | Good use of at least one AI tool; prompts show understanding of techniques covered in the course |
| 2 | Basic use of AI tools; prompts are simple but functional |
| 1 | Minimal AI involvement; appears to have used AI as a last resort |
| 0 | No evidence of meaningful AI tool usage |

---

### 3. Critical Thinking & Evaluation (4 marks)

| Marks | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | Thorough evaluation of AI outputs; clearly identifies strengths, limitations, and what required human correction; reflection is insightful and specific |
| 3 | Good evaluation with clear reflection; identifies key limitations and corrections made |
| 2 | Some evaluation present; reflection is superficial or generic |
| 1 | Very limited reflection; does not meaningfully evaluate AI outputs |
| 0 | No evaluation or reflection provided |

---

### 4. Presentation (4 marks)

| Marks | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | Clear, confident, well-structured 5-minute presentation; covers all required points; handles Q&A well |
| 3 | Good presentation with minor gaps in structure or delivery |
| 2 | Understandable but disorganised or under/over time |
| 1 | Difficult to follow; key content missing |
| 0 | Did not present |

---

### 5. Originality & Personalisation (4 marks)

| Marks | Criteria |
|-------|----------|
| 4 | Unique, personalised approach; clearly tailored to student's own degree, interests, or career goals; goes beyond the minimum requirements |
| 3 | Some personalisation; student's own context is evident |
| 2 | Mostly generic; could have been produced by any student |
| 1 | Appears to be lightly modified from a template with no personal context |
| 0 | No personalisation; copied or plagiarised |

---

## Trainer Notes

- Marks are awarded holistically within each band, not mechanically
- A project that shows excellent critical thinking (4/4) can compensate for a weaker presentation (2/4)
- The reflection is often the most revealing section — students who understand what AI did well vs poorly demonstrate higher-order learning
- For Track D (AI for Your Discipline) projects, apply domain knowledge when evaluating accuracy

## Academic Integrity

AI-assisted work is expected and encouraged for this project. Students are expected to:
- Disclose which AI tools they used
- Edit and personalise AI outputs rather than submitting them verbatim
- Be able to explain every part of their project if asked

Submitting unedited AI output as entirely your own work without disclosure is a violation of the [Code of Conduct](../../CODE-OF-CONDUCT.md).
